Building a Personal Blog from Scratch

After years of publishing on third-party platforms, I decided it was time to build something of my own. Not because I was dissatisfied with existing tools, but because I wanted complete control over my digital presence and the creative freedom to experiment.

Why Build Your Own?

Third-party platforms like Medium and dev.to are excellent for reaching audiences, but they come with limitations. You don't own the design, you're subject to their monetization rules, and your content lives behind their branding. For a personal blog, I wanted something that felt truly mine.

The Technical Choices

I kept things simple: vanilla HTML, CSS, and a flat file structure. No frameworks, no build tools, no database. Just static files served directly. This means fast load times, zero maintenance overhead, and the ability to host anywhere.

Design Philosophy

The design prioritizes readability and focus. Dark theme for late-night writing sessions. Generous whitespace. Typography chosen for long-form reading. No clutter, no distractions—just words on a screen.

Lessons Learned

The process taught me more about web standards than years of working with frameworks. When you strip away the abstractions, you understand how browsers actually work—how the cascade flows, how layout algorithms operate, how accessibility truly functions.

This blog isn't trying to be anything fancy. It's a place for thoughts, experiments, and occasional photography. If you find something useful here, I'm glad. Otherwise, thanks for stopping by.
